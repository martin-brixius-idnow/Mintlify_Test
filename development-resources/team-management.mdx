---
title: Team management
description: Set team member roles and manage access within your organization
---

IDnow uses role-based access control to determine what team members can view and manage within your organization. Each role comes with a predefined set of permissions, ensuring account permissions are appropriately limited.

- **Editor**: Full access to managing flows, sessions, and team members
- **Viewer**: Read-only access with the ability to run flows

---

## Editor

Editors have full control over your organization. Editors can:

- Create, edit and delete flows
- Configure flows settings
- Start and view sessions
- View session history, analytics, and session details
- Manage team members within your organization


**_Managing team members_**

Editors are responsible for controlling access to your organization. They can:

- Invite new team members as editors or viewers
- Remove existing editors or viewers from the organization
- Change a team members’ roles

---

## Viewer

Viewers have read-only access to your organization to monitor activity and test flows built by editors. Viewers can:

- View all flows and content
- Start and view executions
- View session history, analytics, and session details

Viewers can not:

- Modify flows or settings
- Manage team members or roles

## Permissions

Quick reference of team role permissions:

| Action                    | Editor | Viewer |
| ------------------------- | ------ | ------ |
| View flows and content    | ✅     | ✅     |
| Start sessions            | ✅     | ✅     |
| View session              | ✅     | ✅     |
| View session history      | ✅     | ✅     |
| View analytics            | ✅     | ✅     |
| View session details      | ✅     | ✅     |
| Create/edit/delete flows  | ✅     | ❌     |
| Configure flow settings   | ✅     | ❌     |
| Add/remove team members   | ✅     | ❌     |
| Assign roles              | ✅     | ❌     |
