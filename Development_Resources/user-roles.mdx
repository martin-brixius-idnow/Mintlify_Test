title: User roles
description: Set user roles and manage access within your organization
---

The IDnow Trust Platform uses role-based access control to determine what users can view and manage within your organization. Each role comes with a predefined set of permissions, ensuring users have access only to the features they need.

**Editor** — Full access to manage flows, sessions, and users 
**Viewer** — Read-only access with the ability to run flows

---

## Editor

Editors have full control over the Trust Platform . This role is ideal for team members who need to build, configure, and manage flows.

### What Editors Can Do

- Create, edit, and delete flows
- Configure flows settings
- Start and view sessions
- View session history, analytics, and session details
- Manage users within the organization


### Managing Users

Editors are responsible for controlling access to your organization. They can:

- Add new users and assign them the Editor or Viewer role
- Remove existing Editors or Viewers from the organization
- Change a user's role at any time

---

## Viewer

Viewers have read-only access to your organization. This role is ideal for team members who need to monitor activity and run existing flows.

### What Viewers Can Do

- View all flows and content
- Start and view executions
- View session history, analytics, and session details

### What Viewers Cannot Do

- Modify flows or settings
- Manage users or roles

## Permissions Matrix

Below is a comparison of what each role is allowed to do.

| View session               | ✅     | ✅     |
